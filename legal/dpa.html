Data Processing Addendum (DPA)

v1.0. Last updated: 15 March 2026

This Data Processing Addendum ("DPA") is entered into between Clad Technologies ("Clad") and the Customer, and forms part of the Clad Terms of Service ("Agreement"). This DPA applies to the extent Clad processes Personal Data on behalf of Customer in providing the Services.

1. Definitions

Capitalised terms not defined in this DPA have the meaning set out in the Agreement.

  • "Controller", "Processor", "Data Subject", "Personal Data", and "Processing" have the meanings given in the GDPR.
  • "Customer Data" means Personal Data that Customer uploads or provides via the Services.
  • "Sub-processor" means any third party engaged by Clad to Process Customer Data.

2. Roles and Scope

Customer is the Controller and Clad is the Processor of Customer Data. This DPA applies only to Customer Data subject to EU/EEA or UK data protection laws.

3. Processing Instructions

Clad shall process Customer Data only on documented instructions from the Customer. This includes instructions regarding the subject matter, nature, purpose, and duration of the processing, the types of Personal Data, and the categories of Data Subjects.

The processing is carried out on the legal basis defined by the Customer in accordance with Articles 6 and/or 9 of the GDPR, as applicable. Clad processes Personal Data solely based on documented instructions provided by the Customer.

Clad shall not process Customer Data for any other purpose unless required to do so by Union or Member State law to which Clad is subject. In such cases, Clad shall inform the Customer of the legal requirement before processing, unless prohibited from doing so on important grounds of public interest.

If Clad considers that an instruction from the Customer infringes the GDPR or other applicable data protection law, Clad shall promptly inform the Customer and suspend further processing until the instruction is confirmed, amended, or withdrawn.

4. Confidentiality

Clad ensures that all personnel authorised to process Customer Data are bound by confidentiality obligations.

5. Sub-processors

Clad uses Sub-processors listed at clad.io/legal/subprocessors. Clad will:

  • Provide at least 30 days' notice before adding or replacing a Sub-processor;
  • Ensure Sub-processors are bound by equivalent data protection terms.

Customer may object to a new Sub-processor for reasonable grounds related to data protection.

6. Security Measures

Clad implements appropriate technical and organisational measures, including:

  • Encryption at rest and in transit;
  • Logical access controls;
  • Data segregation;
  • Regular vulnerability testing;
  • Incident response procedures.

In the event of a Personal Data Breach, Clad will notify Customer without undue delay and in any case within 72 hours of becoming aware.

7. Data Transfers

For transfers from the UK, the UK International Data Transfer Addendum (version B.1.0) is incorporated. For Switzerland, references to 'Member State' and 'GDPR' in the SCCs shall be interpreted under the Swiss FADP.

Customer Data may be transferred outside the EEA. Clad relies on:

  • Hosting in the EU;
  • Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs);
  • Supplementary safeguards.

8. Assistance & Data Subject Rights

Clad will assist Customer in:

  • Responding to Data Subject requests;
  • Conducting DPIAs and prior consultations.

Requests must be submitted to legal@clad.io.

Clad shall cooperate, upon request, with the relevant supervisory authority in the performance of its tasks, to the extent required by applicable law.

9. Return or Deletion

Upon account deletion, Clad will delete Customer Data within 30 days, unless retention is required by law. Downgrades to a Free plan do not trigger deletion.

Encrypted backups are overwritten on a 35-day rolling cycle.

Upon termination of the Services, Clad shall, at the choice of the Customer, delete or return all Customer Data and delete existing copies unless Union or Member State law requires storage of the personal data.

10. Audit Rights

Customer is entitled to carry out audits and inspections, including on-site audits, at its own cost, once per year or more frequently if required by law or a supervisory authority. Such audits must be subject to reasonable scheduling and confidentiality obligations.

On request, Clad will provide documentation of its compliance. Customers may audit Clad once per year with 30 days' notice, subject to confidentiality.

11. Liability

Each party's liability under this DPA is subject to the limitations set out in the Agreement, except for breaches of this DPA caused by Clad's gross negligence or wilful misconduct.

12. Precedence

If this DPA conflicts with the Agreement, this DPA prevails for data protection matters. The SCCs prevail over this DPA and the Agreement in case of conflict.
