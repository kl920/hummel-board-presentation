Privacy Policy

v1.0. Last updated: 15 March 2026

Clad collects and uses Personal Data to provide your organisation with the Clad platform (the "Services") — a system for AI-powered fashion photography, content-production workflows, and intelligent automation — as well as all related services and integrations.

This Privacy Policy applies to all users of the Services, including API access, regardless of plan. This Privacy Policy also applies to clad.io (the "Website") and any other interactions you might have with Clad, such as customer service requests. If you do not accept this Privacy Policy, please refrain from using the Services, Website, or any part of Clad's operations.

Additionally, a separate agreement (the "Terms of Service") governs the delivery, access, and use of the Services, including the handling of data such as media, files, or other content submitted through Services accounts (collectively referred to as "Customer Data"). The organisation (such as your employer or another entity or individual) that entered into the Terms of Service (the "Customer") manages its instance of the Services and all associated Customer Data.

California Notice of Collection of Personal Information: We collect the information described below under "Information Clad Collects" for the business and commercial purposes described below under "Use of Personal Data."

1. Information Clad Collects

Log Data

When you visit Clad's website, servers automatically log standard browser data (IP address, browser type/version, pages visited, timestamps, and related diagnostics).

Device Data

Clad collects device information when you access the Services or Website, subject to your browser or device settings.

Personal Information

You may provide your name, email address, work address, and website.

Business Data

Data generated during use of the Clad platform, such as asset metadata, product records, workflow logs, and analytics.

We collect Personal Data in three ways:

  • Directly from you when you fill out forms, register an account, or contact us
  • Automatically, via cookies and similar technologies (see Cookie Policy)
  • From the Customer, if your employer or organisation provides your information as part of a Services account

2. Clad's Role as Data Processor and Controller

Clad acts as a data processor on behalf of Customers when processing Customer Data submitted via the Services. The Customer is the data controller in this context.

For other types of data, Clad acts as the data controller, including:

  • Website usage (clad.io)
  • Support and sales interactions
  • Account creation and administration
  • Product analytics and feature optimisation
  • Marketing communications (if consented)

Clad does not use Customer Data for its own purposes without written instructions or consent from the Customer.

DPO Statement

Clad's core activities do not involve large-scale processing of special-category data or systematic monitoring. Therefore, a Data Protection Officer is not required under GDPR Art 37. Privacy inquiries: legal@clad.io

Consent can be withdrawn at any time. Past processing remains lawful.

4. Use of Personal Data

Clad uses Personal Data to:

  • Provide core platform features
  • Communicate with users
  • Maintain internal records
  • Conduct analytics and improve services
  • Perform advertising and marketing, only with consent

Use of Data for AI Feature Training (Free, Starter, and Team Plans)

In short: Free/Starter/Team data may improve models; Pro data never trains models unless you opt in.

Clad uses anonymised platform data from Free, Starter, and Team plans to improve AI model accuracy and feature quality. This processing does not apply to Pro plans. Training datasets are aggregated, de-identified, and stored separately from production systems.

Safeguards:

  • No customer's data is exposed to others
  • Models are not fine-tuned to memorise or leak proprietary information

Legal basis: Legitimate interest, with protective measures in place.

Automated Decision-Making

Clad's AI may apply suggestions (e.g., tagging or categorisation) in a staging area only. No data is published or synced externally without human review. A user must confirm and approve any changes before they reach production.

Clad's AI features never produce automated decisions with legal or similarly significant effects under GDPR Article 22. All outputs require user review and approval before publication or integration.

AI Transparency

If our Services include AI features subject to the EU AI Act or other applicable laws, Clad will comply with any required transparency, logging, or documentation obligations.

5. Sharing Data with Third Parties

Clad shares Personal Data with third-party service providers (e.g., hosting, analytics, support tools). All subprocessors are bound by strict agreements. Before adding or replacing any subprocessor, we will:

  1. Perform a security and privacy assessment.
  2. Provide at least 7 days' notice before adding or replacing a sub-processor.
  3. Publish an up-to-date subprocessor list at clad.io/legal/subprocessors.

6. International Transfers

Clad stores Customer Data and Personal Data in the EU/EEA. If data is transferred outside this region — for example, to subprocessors or service providers in the United States — Clad uses legally approved safeguards, such as:

  • Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs)
  • The UK's International Data Transfer Agreement (IDTA)
  • Adequacy decisions (if applicable)

For a list of subprocessors and transfer locations, see: Sub-Processor List.

7. Your Rights

As a data subject, you have the following rights under applicable data protection laws:

  • Access – You can request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
  • Rectification – You can ask us to correct inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • Erasure – You can request that we delete your data, subject to legal limits.
  • Restriction – You can ask us to limit how we use your data in certain cases.
  • Objection – You can object to our processing if it is based on legitimate interests.
  • Portability – You can request your data in a portable format for reuse elsewhere.
  • Withdrawal of Consent – If we process data based on your consent, you can withdraw it at any time. This does not affect past processing.

To exercise any of these rights, email us at legal@clad.io with "DSAR Request" in the subject line and include relevant details. We will acknowledge your request within 5 business days and respond within 30 days.

You also have the right to lodge a complaint with your local data protection authority. In Denmark, contact Datatilsynet — https://www.datatilsynet.dk
